# Hypotheses

## AL AMIN

**H1:** We believe that modular onboarding journeys will help operation analysts because they currently feel overwhelmed by access to systems without knowing which are role-relevant.

**H2:** We believe that a single Knowledge Hub will help cross-functional employees because they currently waste time checking multiple fragmented sources.

**H3:** We believe that AI source-linking will help workers in large organizations because their biggest frustration is not knowing if information is the "latest version".

## ENOSCH

**H1:** We think a centralised dashboard is an idea for new employees. It makes sense because it reduces the need to switch between tools. This way information is easier to access for employees.

**H2:** We think a guided onboarding flow is an idea for new users. It should take the next steps. This is because new users currently feel unsure about what to do after completing tasks.

**H3:** We think showing document sources and updated timestamps is a good idea for users. This is because users will trust information more. Now users rely on trial and error to verify accuracy of information.

**H4:** We think personalised onboarding is an idea for new employees. For example, it could be role-based content or buddy system integration. This is because current onboarding feels generic and incomplete for employees.

**H5:** We think a prioritised task view is an idea for users. It should show deadlines and urgent tasks, and key actions. This is because users struggle with prioritization and tight deadlines.

## Astha

**H1:** New retail employees feel overwhelmed during onboarding due to the need to learn multiple tasks (e.g. till operations, store layout, and customer service) within a short period of time without structured guidance.

**H2:** Employees rely heavily on colleagues for information due to the absence of a centralised system that provides accurate and consistent answers.

**H3:** A lack of clear onboarding structure (such as step-by-step guides or checklists) leads to confusion around daily tasks and priorities.

**H4:** Employees experience low confidence during their first few weeks due to limited hands-on training and unclear expectations.

**H5:** Employees are more likely to trust AI tools when the information provided is transparent, accurate, and supported with clear explanations or sources.

## JOY

**H1:** I believe that a dashboard composed of schedules, deadlines, course content, and students contacts, faculty members' contacts will help lecturers and employees because they currently check multiple sources and people, which makes other work delayed and causes perplex situation.

**H2:** Evidence suggests that guided onboarding experience with clear daily tasks and next steps will help new employees because users do not know what to do in their first week and feel lost without structured guidance.

**H3:** It is anticipated that role-specific, hands-on onboarding will help new employees, current onboarding feels like "box-ticking" and does not focus on actual job responsibilities.

**H4:** I consider that a single source of truth with verified information will help employees make decisions faster, because currently employees receive inconsistent answers from different people and must repeatedly cross-check information.

**H5:** An AI assistant that verify sources and explanations will help employees trust answers more because employees do not trust AI unless they can verify where the information comes from.
